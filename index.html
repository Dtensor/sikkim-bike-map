Signature rides from Kalimpong

~210 km loop

1 · Old Silk Route

Kalimpong → Algarah → Pedong → Rhenock → Rongli 🛂 → Zuluk (32 hairpins) → Thambi → Nathang → Kupup → Tsomgo → Gangtok. The greatest hairpin stack in the East Himalaya.

~450 km, 4–5 days

2 · North Sikkim Grand Tour

Kalimpong → Gangtok → Mangan ⛽ (last reliable fuel) → Chungthang → split: Lachen–Gurudongmar (5,183 m) and Lachung–Yumthang–Zero Point. Carry spare fuel from Mangan.

~280 km loop

3 · West Sikkim Monastery Loop

Kalimpong → Melli → Jorethang → Namchi (Char Dham) → Ravangla (Buddha Park) → Legship → Pelling (Pemayangtse) → Dentam → Uttarey, return via Tashiding–Yuksom side trip.

~120 km loop

4 · Kalimpong Hill Circuit

Kalimpong → Lava → Loleygaon (Canopy Walk) → back via Algarah; add Gorubathan descent to the Dooars if you want a fast riverside run home along NH10.

~110 km

5 · Nathu La Dash

Gangtok → Tsomgo Lake ◉ → Nathu La (4,310 m, Indo-China border) 🛂. Permit issued in Gangtok only; closed Mon–Tue; Indians only.

Permits & ground rules

Restricted zones (shaded on map): everything beyond Rongli (East / Silk Route) and beyond Mangan (North) needs a Protected Area Permit — arranged in a day via any Gangtok/Rongli tour agent; personal bikes allowed on Silk Route & North Sikkim, but Gurudongmar & Nathu La often require a registered local vehicle/guide — verify current rules in Gangtok. Foreign nationals: North & East Sikkim need PAP in groups of 2+; Nathu La is off-limits.
Fuel discipline: reliable pumps at Kalimpong, Melli, Rangpo, Singtam, Gangtok, Jorethang, Namchi, Ravangla, Gyalshing, Mangan, Rongli. Nothing after Mangan (north) or Rongli (east). Gurudongmar round trip from Lachen ≈ 135 km — carry 5 L spare.
Season: Mar–May & Oct–Dec best. Monsoon (Jun–Sep) = landslides on NH10 and Mangan–Chungthang. Dec–Feb: snow above 3,000 m; Zero Point & Gurudongmar may shut. Start riding by 7 am — mountain fog owns the afternoons.
